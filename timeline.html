Pandemic History

A chronological journey through major viral outbreaks that shaped our world

COVID-19 Pandemic

2019 - Present

Caused by SARS-CoV-2, this pandemic reshaped global society, economies, and healthcare systems. Originating in Wuhan, China, it spread globally within months.

Coronavirus | Global Impact

Ebola Outbreak

2014 - 2016

The most widespread epidemic of Ebola virus disease in history, primarily affecting Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone in West Africa.

High Fatality | West Africa

H1N1 Swine Flu

2009

A new influenza virus that spread worldwide. It contained a combination of flu genes not previously seen in animals or people.

Influenza | Vaccine Developed

HIV/AIDS Recognition

1981

The beginning of the AIDS pandemic. The virus attacks the immune system, leading to life-threatening opportunistic infections.

Retrovirus | Treatment Available

Hong Kong Flu (H3N2)

1968

A category 2 flu pandemic that originated in Hong Kong. It spread to the United States and caused an estimated 1-4 million deaths worldwide.

Influenza A | 1-4M Deaths

Spanish Flu

1918

The deadliest pandemic in modern history. Caused by H1N1 virus, it infected 500 million people and killed 50 million worldwide.

50M Deaths | Worldwide
